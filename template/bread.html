Why add Sourdough Bread to your diet?Sourdough bread is a traditional type of bread made by fermenting the dough using naturally occurring lactobacilli and yeast. This method of preparation dates back thousands of years and is known for its distinctive tangy flavor, chewy texture, and crisp crust. The fermentation process not only imparts a taste but also offers several health benefits, making sourdough bread a Sourdough is good for you.


Easier Digestion:

The fermentation process breaks down gluten, which can make sourdough bread easier to digest for people with mild gluten sensitivities. It's not gluten-free but is often better tolerated than standard wheat bread. Improved Nutrient Absorption:

Fermentation helps to break down the phytic acid found in flour, which can inhibit the absorption of minerals. This means that the body can more easily absorb the nutrients from sourdough bread, such as iron, zinc, and magnesium. Lower Glycemic Index:

Sourdough fermentation can lower the bread's glycemic index (GI), resulting in a slower release of glucose into the bloodstream. This can help in managing blood sugar levels, making it a healthier option for people with diabetes and those looking to maintain stable energy levels.


No Added Preservatives:
